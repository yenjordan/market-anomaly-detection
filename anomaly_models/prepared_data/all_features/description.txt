Strategy 1: All Features

Purpose:
- Provide comprehensive market analysis
- Capture cross-asset relationships
- Maximize available information for anomaly detection

Features:
Base Features:
- VIX (CBOE Volatility Index)
- MXUS, MXEU, MXJP, MXCN (Equity indices for US, Europe, Japan, China)
- XAU BGNL (Gold price index)
- Cl1 (Crude Oil futures)
- CRY (Thomson Reuters/CoreCommodity CRB Index)
- DXY (US Dollar Index)
- USGG30YR, USGG2YR (US Treasury yields)
- GT10 (US 10-year Treasury yield)
- GTDEM30YR, GTDEM2YR, GTDEM10Y (German Treasury yields)
- GTJPY10YR (Japanese 10-year yield)
- LUACTRUU (Bloomberg US Aggregate Bond Index)
- BDIY (Baltic Dry Index)
- JPY (Japanese Yen exchange rate)

Derived Features:
- Cross-asset correlations
- Rolling statistics
- Market stress indicators
- Dispersion metrics
- Yield curve metrics
- Volatility regime indicators

Advantages:
- Maximum information utilization
- Captures complex market interactions
- Robust to different types of stress
- Comprehensive risk coverage

Disadvantages:
- High dimensionality
- Complex feature interactions
- Risk of overfitting
- Computationally intensive
